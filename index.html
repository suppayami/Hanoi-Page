General: Hanoi General Information Located at the center cof the Red River, Hanoi capital – the city inside three rivers now not only the country’s center of economy, politics, culture, and society, but also the biggest city in Vietnam and one of 17 biggest capital of the world with around 8 million people, and an area of 3.325 square km. Hanoi is also well known for cultural, historical and traditional values....
Hoan Kiem lake: Situated in the heart of Hanoi, the group structure Thap Rua (Turtle Tower) and Ngoc island with Ngoc Son temple, Pen tower and The Huc bridge have since become prominent city icons. http://xomnhiepanh.com/uploads/gallery/2011/03/91270_1301372404.jpg Temple of Literature - Van Mieu Quoc Tu Giam: The oldest and possibly the finest architectural complex in Hanoi, Van Mieu or Temple of Literature, was established in 1070, has became a original center for higher learning, educating future mandarins for more than seven centuries. http://xuhuongdulich.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/van-mieu-quoc-tu-giam.jpg Hanoi Old Quarter: Hanoi's Old Quarter, or 36 Pho Phuong (36 Streets), with over a thousand years of history, remains one of Vietnam's most lively and unusual places by small artisans and merchants. http://static.panoramio.com/photos/original/88241288.jpg
1. Pho Phở is just one of many, many, MANY noodle soups in Vietnam. From a strictly linguistic standpoint, phở refers to the rice noodles, not the soup itself. But it's become synonymous with the staple soup served with various meat parts (usually beef or chicken), bean sprouts, lime wedges, the essential greens (basil, mint, cilantro, and onions), and whatever chili sauce and fish sauce you need to doctor up the broth to your liking. http://www.seriouseats.com/images/2012/05/20120514-206374-hanoi-pho-bo-primary.jpg http://www.seriouseats.com/images/2013/01/20130121-vietnamese-dishes05.jpg 2. Bun cha You really can't go to Hanoi without trying bún chả. It comes with grilled pork sausage patties, a basket of herbs, bean sprouts, pickled veggies, and, once again, the ever-important nước chấm sauce (pour it over everything). http://www.seriouseats.com/images/2013/01/20130121-vietnamese-dishes02.jpg 3. Banh cuon The classic Hanoi preparation: a soft, delicately chewy steamed roll filled with minced pork and mushrooms, topped with crunchy fried shallots, and served with springy, slightly sweet slabs of cinnamon-y pork sausage. Each bite is topped with deep-fried shallot bits and must be dipped into that nước chấm sauce. http://www.seriouseats.com/images/2013/01/20130121-vietnamese-dishes09.jpg 4. Cha ca Chunks of fish are anointed with plenty of turmeric, then sizzled with heaps of dill and served with rice noodles. It’s a fragrant delight. http://www.transindus.co.uk/sites/default/files/cha-ca-la-vong.jpg 5. Street food Street food can be found on the main streets of Hanoi, and down many of the side streets and alleways. //bia hơi, chè, nem chua rán, khoai tây chiên
